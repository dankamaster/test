# Competitor Teardown

## 1. EUAA — Country of Origin Information
Type: institutional methodology source.

Strengths:
- High authority.
- Strong COI definition and methodology.
- Strong source ecosystem.

Gaps:
- EU-focused, not US attorney-facing.
- No email-first workflow.
- Not a conversion-ready web asset for law firms.

How we outperform:
- US asylum context.
- Attorney-controlled workflow.
- COI report structure, checklist, CTA, FAQ, schema, PWA.

## 2. CGRS / UC Law SF
Type: practical US asylum/country conditions authority.

Strengths:
- Practical country conditions manual.
- Expert witness database.
- High relevance to asylum evidence.

Gaps:
- PDF/manual/database format.
- Not web-first.
- Not conversion-optimized for legal teams.

How we outperform:
- Web-first article.
- Scannable H2/H3.
- Email request flow.
- Privacy-safe popup.

## 3. Commercial/service competitor: CountryConditions.expert / Diverse Legal / Ereventis
Type: B2B expert/service intent.

Strengths:
- Close commercial language.
- Expert/country report positioning.

Gaps:
- Often lacks strong source-backed educational framework.
- Limited SEO/PWA/editorial transparency.
- May not clearly separate documentation support from legal advice.

How we outperform:
- Expert guide + documentation pathway.
- Legal/YMYL guardrails.
- Source-backed workflow.
- Email-first CTA.

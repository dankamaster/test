# Editorial Policy and EEAT Notes

## Purpose

This project is an educational, attorney-facing SEO/PWA resource about Country of Origin Information reports, country conditions packets, and expert documentation support for US asylum matters.

The page does not provide legal advice and does not create an attorney-client relationship.

## Editorial responsibility

The visible page uses an `Editorial Team` author placeholder because this is a test assignment prototype. A production version should replace this with:

- named author;
- role and biography;
- source review responsibility;
- qualified immigration attorney reviewer, if available;
- last reviewed date;
- organization contact details.

## Legal boundary

The page must preserve the attorney-controlled workflow:

- counsel determines legal strategy;
- counsel determines protected ground, procedural posture, relevance, filing strategy, and final use of evidence;
- the documentation team supports country conditions research, source organization, citations, packet structure, and draft materials for attorney review.

## Source policy

Source-sensitive claims should be supported by primary or high-trust sources. Preferred hierarchy:

1. US official legal/process sources: USCIS, EOIR/DOJ, eCFR, US Code.
2. International/refugee authorities: UNHCR, Refworld.
3. COI methodology sources: EUAA, UK Home Office, recognized asylum research bodies.
4. Practical legal-aid resources: CGRS, Tahirih, Immigration Equality, Immigration Justice Campaign, NIJC.
5. Commercial competitor pages: used for positioning analysis, not for legal claims.

## AI-assisted workflow

AI may be used for:

- SERP clustering;
- outline generation;
- first-pass drafting;
- source inventory structuring;
- technical scaffolding;
- QA checklist creation.

Manual review is required for:

- legal/YMYL wording;
- source selection;
- attorney-controlled workflow boundaries;
- privacy and cookie language;
- final claims and CTA wording.

## Known production limitations

Before production release, replace all placeholders and add:

- real author/reviewer names;
- real privacy policy URL;
- real contact inbox;
- final internal links;
- Lighthouse screenshots after deployment;
- actual source URLs and access dates in the visible article.

## Keyword Planner insight

Exact COI/country-conditions queries are narrow and low-volume, which is expected for a legal B2B/YMYL topic. Adjacent commercial terms such as `expert witness`, `expert report`, `documentation support`, `legal team`, and `country expert report` show measurable intent and commercial value.

Additional Keyword Planner data:

- `country expert report`: 50 avg. monthly searches, low competition.
- `country conditions expert witness`: no public volume.
- `immigration expert report`: no public volume.
- `asylum country expert report`: no public volume.
- `country conditions documentation`: no public volume.

## SERP / competitor summary

Full competitor teardown: [`docs/competitor-teardown.md`](competitor-teardown.md)

Three competitor groups reviewed:

1. EUAA / official COI methodology resources — strong authority and methodology depth, but not written as a practical US asylum attorney-facing conversion page.
2. CGRS / UC Law SF and legal-aid resources — strong legal-aid credibility and useful practitioner orientation, but not positioned as a dedicated COI report preparation guide.
3. Commercial expert-report / documentation services — commercial intent and service positioning, but often weaker on transparent source methodology, attorney-review boundaries, and YMYL traceability.

Opportunity: outperform with a focused attorney-facing guide that combines practical COI workflow, source hierarchy, citation-table structure, attorney-review boundaries, source limitations, CTA clarity, and reviewer-visible claim-to-source documentation.

## Page angle

A practical guide for legal teams preparing COI reports, country conditions packets, and expert documentation for attorney review in US asylum matters.

The page should avoid applicant-facing promises and stay within an attorney-reviewed documentation support frame: source organization, evidence structure, citation workflow, and research limitations rather than legal advice or outcome prediction.

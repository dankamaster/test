Dominant SERP Pattern

The SERP was not dominated by ordinary blog posts. It mixed institutional COI methodology, legal-aid manuals, law-library guides, expert-witness materials, PDF resources, and specialist service providers.

Research insight: The opportunity is a hybrid page: a scannable educational guide plus an attorney-facing documentation request path.

Source and PDF Layer

The PDF layer mattered because many high-value resources in this topic are manuals, guides, packets, and sample expert materials rather than conventional landing pages.

  • CGRS informed practical country conditions evidence workflow.
  • Tahirih informed expert witness, affidavit, and sample packet framing.
  • EUAA informed COI methodology, but not US legal authority.
  • NIJC and legal-aid resources informed evidence packet and documentation framing.

See PDF Source Library and Source Validation Matrix.

Keyword and Intent Layer

The project targets a low-volume but high-intent legal B2B cluster. The exact primary phrase country of origin information report asylum was added to the article after review because the first version under-targeted its own primary keyword.

  • Primary: country of origin information report asylum.
  • Secondary: country conditions evidence, country conditions packet, US asylum case, expert witness report, attorney review.
  • Commercial modifiers: documentation support, legal teams, expert declaration, expert affidavit.

Legal and YMYL Controls

The page uses attorney-controlled language. It does not claim to provide legal advice, determine eligibility, select legal strategy, or guarantee outcomes.

  • Counsel controls legal strategy and final use of evidence.
  • The documentation team supports research, source organization, citations, and draft materials.
  • Privacy copy warns users not to send sensitive identifiers or confidential details through unsecured email.

See Editorial Policy and Source Methodology.

Implementation and QA Layer

The project uses a static stack to keep the assignment fast, inspectable, and easy to audit. Automated checks cover repository structure, HTML, schema, privacy UI, and accessibility smoke tests.

  • Static HTML/CSS/JS.
  • JSON-LD: Article, WebPage, Organization, Person, BreadcrumbList, FAQPage.
  • PWA basics: manifest, service worker, offline page.
  • Privacy-first cookie preferences: no analytics or marketing scripts by default.

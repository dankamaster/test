AI workflow architecture

Detailed Model Architecture and Review Gates

This page documents the layered AI-assisted workflow used to build, audit, harden, and prepare the COI SEO/PWA test assignment.

This is an internal reviewer artifact. It explains how AI was directed, constrained, audited, and corrected by human review.

Operating Principle

The model was not used as a single-pass writer. It was used as a structured research, drafting, implementation, QA, and red-team system. Each layer had a defined role, risk boundary, and acceptance gate.

Layer Stack

Model layer stack used during the assignment
LayerPurposeAcceptance gate
Brief LockTranslate assignment requirements into deliverables and constraints.Every output maps to Part 1–4 of the brief.
SERP/KeywordIdentify intent, competitors, related queries, and content gaps.Keyword, ICP, CTA, title/meta/outline documented.
Source/YMYLSeparate legal authority, methodology, legal-aid guidance, PDFs, and competitor evidence.Claim-to-source map and legal boundary language present.
ContentDraft expert-oriented English article without filler or legal advice.One H1, clear H2 structure, FAQ, checklist, source note, AI/manual note.
Conversion/PrivacyDesign CTA and form flow without collecting risky case facts.No marketing opt-in in legal form; no confidential facts requested.
Technical SEOCanonical, schema, OG/Twitter, sitemap, robots, internal links.Automated schema/link checks plus manual URL alignment.
PWA/UXManifest, service worker, offline page, sticky mobile header, install prompt.PWA basics visible and non-blocking.
QA/Red TeamRun GitHub Actions, Codex/Claude review, and regression fixes.No merge blockers before merge.

Self-Review Questions

  • Does this satisfy the original brief, not only look impressive?
  • Is every legal/YMYL statement bounded and source-aware?
  • Can a reviewer trace decisions from SERP/source data to page changes?
  • Does the live URL match canonical and sitemap?
  • Can the page fail at runtime on pages missing optional UI elements?
  • Does the form avoid collecting sensitive case details?
  • Are evidence pages clearly marked as internal review artifacts?

Known Remaining Production Boundaries

For the test assignment, placeholders such as Editorial Team and test@test.com are acceptable. For production, these must be replaced with named author/reviewer attribution, real contact details, a real privacy policy, and deployed Lighthouse screenshots.
